LabelLord 2.3 (labeling robot)

LabelLord 2.3 is a labeling robot I designed and built using TinkerCAD as part of a collaborative engineering project. Working with my team, we identified a real-world packaging problem and developed a system of automated robots to solve it. This robot was my individual contribution to the system, featuring a servo-based labeling mechanism and sensor-triggered automation. View the design here.

Electroic Circuit (working simulation)

This TinkerCAD simulation showcases the electronic circuit and Arduino code I developed for LabelLord 2.3. The circuit integrates ultrasonic and IR sensors with a servo motor to create a responsive labeling system. I wrote the control logic in Arduino, enabling automated package detection and label application. View the simulation here.

My Mission

I’m a curious and driven aspiring UX designer who sees every project and challenge as an opportunity to learn and grow. My journey, from earning a GED to maintaining a 4.0 GPA at Arizona State, reflects a deep commitment to my education. I also believe in the power of persistence. I’ve worked hard to be where I am, and I carry that same determination into every part of my design work.

I’m passionate about creating digital experiences that are thoughtful, intuitive, and grounded in real user needs. With a strong foundation in front-end web development and a design thinking mindset, I approach problems with empathy, creativity, and strategic focus.

I’m constantly seeking out new tools, methods, and ideas to improve my work and myself. More than anything, I care about building things that are both functional and meaningful, and I’m always ready to keep learning along the way.
